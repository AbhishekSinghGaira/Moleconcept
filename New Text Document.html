Mole Concept Cheat Sheet

Formulas & Concepts

Topic Formula / Concept Quick Tip
Moles (\(n\)) \( n = \frac{\text{Given Mass (g)}}{\text{Molar Mass (g/mol)}} \) Ensure consistent units (mass in grams).
Particles (\(N\)) \( N = n \times N_A \), \( N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23} \) Avogadro's number for particles.
Volume at STP \( V = n \times V_m \), \( V_m = 22.4 \, \text{L/mol} \) Applies to ideal gases.
Ideal Gas Law \( PV = nRT \) \( R = 0.0821 \, \text{L·atm/(mol·K)} \).
Molarity (\(M\)) \( M = \frac{\text{Moles of Solute}}{\text{Volume (L)}} \) Volume must be in liters.
Molality (\(m\)) \( m = \frac{\text{Moles of Solute}}{\text{Mass of Solvent (kg)}} \) Works best for dilute solutions.
Empirical Formula 1. Find moles of each element.
2. Divide by smallest mole.
3. Adjust to whole numbers.		 Shows simplest ratio of atoms.
Molecular Formula \( \text{MF} = \text{EF} \times n, \, n = \frac{\text{Molar Mass}}{\text{EF Mass}} \) \( n \) must be an integer.
Percentage Composition \( \%\text{Element} = \frac{\text{Mass of Element}}{\text{Molar Mass}} \times 100 \) Useful for empirical formula.

Special Cases

Case Key Relation Application
Dalton’s Law \( P_{\text{total}} = \sum P_i \), \( P_i = X_i \cdot P_{\text{total}} \) Gas mixtures.
Limiting Reagent Smallest \( \frac{\text{Moles}}{\text{Coefficient}} \) Determines product formed.
Density of Gas \( \rho = \frac{PM}{RT} \) Relates \( P, M, T \).

Constants

  • Avogadro’s Number (\(N_A\)): \( 6.022 \times 10^{23} \, \text{particles/mol} \)
  • Molar Volume (\(V_m\)) at STP: \( 22.4 \, \text{L/mol} \)
  • Universal Gas Constant (\(R\)): \( 0.0821 \, \text{L·atm/(mol·K)} \) or \( 8.314 \, \text{J/(mol·K)} \)
  • STP Conditions: \( T = 273 \, \text{K}, P = 1 \, \text{atm} \)

Pro Tips

  • Always balance equations for stoichiometry.
  • Use dimensional analysis to confirm unit consistency.
  • Practice problems covering all cases: gas laws, solutions, and stoichiometry.
