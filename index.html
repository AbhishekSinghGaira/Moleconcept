Mole Concept Cheat Sheet

Formulas & Concepts

Topic Formula / Concept Quick Tip
Moles (\(n\)) n = Given Mass (g) ÷ Molar Mass (g/mol) Ensure consistent units (mass in grams).
Particles (\(N\)) N = n × NA, NA = 6.022 × 1023 Avogadro's number for particles.
Volume at STP V = n × Vm, Vm = 22.4 L/mol Applies to ideal gases.
Ideal Gas Law PV = nRT R = 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K).
Molarity (\(M\)) M = Moles of Solute ÷ Volume (L) Volume must be in liters.
Molality (\(m\)) m = Moles of Solute ÷ Mass of Solvent (kg) Works best for dilute solutions.
Empirical Formula 1. Find moles of each element.
2. Divide by smallest mole.
3. Adjust to whole numbers.		 Shows simplest ratio of atoms.
Molecular Formula MF = EF × n, n = Molar Mass ÷ EF Mass n must be an integer.
Percentage Composition %Element = (Mass of Element ÷ Molar Mass) × 100 Useful for empirical formula.

Special Cases

Case Key Relation Application
Dalton’s Law Ptotal = ΣPi, Pi = Xi · Ptotal Gas mixtures.
Limiting Reagent Smallest (Moles ÷ Coefficient) Determines product formed.
Density of Gas ρ = PM ÷ RT Relates P, M, T.

Constants

  • Avogadro’s Number (NA): 6.022 × 1023 particles/mol
  • Molar Volume (Vm) at STP: 22.4 L/mol
  • Universal Gas Constant (R): 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K) or 8.314 J/(mol·K)
  • STP Conditions: T = 273 K, P = 1 atm

Pro Tips

  • Always balance equations for stoichiometry.
  • Use dimensional analysis to confirm unit consistency.
  • Practice problems covering all cases: gas laws, solutions, and stoichiometry.
